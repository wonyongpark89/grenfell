Design Your Learning Experience

A structured planning tool for educators preparing to teach about the Grenfell Tower fire.

20 minutes 5 sections Copy or print output

Work through each section to build an initial plan for your lesson or scheme of work. Select your focus areas, draft your thinking, and export the result at the end. This is a private working document — your notes are not submitted or stored.

1. Learners and Context
4 mins
Who are your learners and what is their starting point?
Consider: age and key stage; subject context; prior knowledge of Grenfell or related topics (housing, regulation, community); potential personal connections to the fire, to tower blocks, or to housing insecurity; any known sensitivities or safeguarding considerations.
2. Focus Areas
3 mins
Which aspects of the Grenfell story will you prioritise?
Select all that apply. You do not need to cover everything — depth on a few themes is more effective than breadth across all of them.
Knowledge and Understanding
Skills and Disposition
3. Learning Objectives
4 mins
Draft objectives across three domains: Knowledge, Skills, and Values.
Knowledge: What facts, contexts or relationships must learners understand?
Skills: What will they be able to do — e.g., analyse, evaluate, compare, argue?
Values: What dispositions should the learning develop — e.g., empathy, a sense of justice, respect for affected communities?
4. Creating a Safe Space
4 mins
How will you manage the sensitivity of this material?
Consider: ground rules for discussion; content warnings and opt-out provisions; 'distancing' techniques (e.g., using published testimony rather than asking for personal disclosure); how you will handle unexpected emotional responses; signposting to pastoral support.
5. Activity Idea
5 mins
Draft one core activity or task.
Think about: the type of activity (source enquiry, structured discussion, case study, creative response); what materials learners will work with; what the output or assessment looks like; and how this activity connects to your objectives above.
